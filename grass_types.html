Grass Types

 

Bermuda Varieties

  • Improved Common Bermuda is a very durable grass for lawns. It has the ability to withstand heat very well and tolerates cold weather also. During the cool season, it will turn brown as this is the dormant nature of this grass. Bermuda grass will spread rapidly if watered and fertilized. This grass requires less maintenance (other than mowing) than almost any other grass. It grows best in full sun.

  • Astro Bermuda is a variety which we have selected to grow because it is more disease-resistant than other varieties. Some characteristics are a medium-sized blade and it maintains a beautiful dark-green color. This grass, like all Bermudas, likes sun and spreads very rapidly to give good coverage if water, fertilizer and mowing are applied on a regular basis.

  • 419 Tiffway Bermuda is a fine bladed strain of Bermuda grass noted for its compact growth (sometimes referred to as dwarf). It is often selected for fine homes and golf course fairways as a “show-case” grass. To maintain the best appearance, grass clippings must be picked up when mowing. The use of water, fertilizer and regular mowing are needed for this grass to thrive just as in other Bermudas. This is a sun-loving grass and can tolerate both heat and cold temperatures.

Fescue Varieties

  • Black Beauty Knowing that over half of the homes in any city have trees, we set out to find a shade loving grass that would provide an green lawn year-round. Johnathan Green suppies us with grass seed that is superior to any other variety we have grown before. Water is essential for fescue grass as well as special care in fertilizing and mowing.

Zoysia Varieties

  • Meyer Zoysia is a carpet-like grass that will tolerate slightly more shade than the Bermudas, but it is much slower at spreading than Bermuda. Zoysia has a dense root system and to maintain the best appearance, clippings need to be picked up while mowing. Due to its extreme hardiness, it is being used more and more on golf courses as well as fine homes. Regular watering, mowing and fertilization make this an outstanding lawn grass.
  • Matrella Zoysia has a softer “feel” as it has a finer blade than the Meyer Zoysia. Like Meyer, it does not spread as fast as Bermuda, but it is just as tough as or tougher than Bermuda. Its dense root system requires regular watering, fertilizer and mowing (with clippings removed), but you will be rewarded with a beautiful, outstanding lawn.

Buffalograss

  • 609 Buffalograss was developed by Nebraska University Horticulture Department. It requires less water, less fertilizer, and less mowing. It can accommodate poor soil conditions and survive very well. This is a tough grass that will do well in neglected areas.
